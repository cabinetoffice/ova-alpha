Prove your identity with a GOV.UK account

You can currently only prove your identity with your GOV.UK account if you have a UK passport.

{{ govukInsetText({ text: "If your passport has expired, you can still use it to prove your identity up until 18 months after the expiry date." }) }}

You do not need to have your passport on you if you can remember the details on it.

What you need to do

It will take you about 10 minutes to prove your identity this way.

{{ govukInsetText({ text: "This service is currently only available in English." }) }}

You'll need to create or sign in to your GOV.UK account first. We'll then ask you for your current address and passport details.

We'll also ask you some security questions that only you should know the answers to. You might find it easier to answer these questions if you have information about:

  • your mobile phone contract
  • your bank account
  • any credit card, loans or mortgages yiu have

We'll only use the information you give us to make sure that you are who you say you are.

Would you like to prove your identity another way?
If you are unable to upload any of the necessary documents then you can also verify your identity by post or in person at your local drop in centre.
